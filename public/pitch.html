THE PROBLEM

Paid ads are a
guessing game.

$190B wasted annually

Brands spend on ads that don't convert. Marketing interns test 3 creatives, pick the "best" one, and call it a day. No systematic experimentation.

CPC vs CAC confusion

Teams optimize for cheap clicks (low CPC) instead of actual customers (low CAC). A viral reel with $0.10 CPC but 0% conversion is worthless.

Creative fatigue is invisible

Ads that worked last month stop working. By the time you notice, you've burned thousands. There's no feedback loop — just vibes.

Manual iteration is slow

Testing a new hook angle takes a week to brief, produce, launch, and analyze. A startup running 3 tests/month is moving at a crawl.

KEY INSIGHT

CPC is vanity.
CAC is reality.

CPC
Cost Per Click

What you pay for each click.
Low CPC = cheap attention.
But attention != customers.
A curiosity-hook reel gets tons of clicks at $0.15 CPC — but nobody buys.

CAC
Cost to Acquire Customer

What you pay for each real customer.
Low CAC = efficient growth.
This is the only metric that matters.
A pain-point reel gets fewer clicks at $0.80 CPC — but 4% convert.

HookLoop optimizes for CAC, not CPC. The bandit kills high-CPC-but-low-conversion reels automatically.
